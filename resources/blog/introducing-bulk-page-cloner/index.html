Re-introducing Bulk Page Cloner for Confluence

By Eric Huang  ·  May 18, 2026  ·  2 min read

Confluence makes it easy to create one page. But if you need ten, twenty, or twenty-five — all named differently and placed in the right spot — you're stuck clicking duplicate, renaming, moving, and repeating.

Bulk Page Cloner was built to fix exactly that. It's a Confluence Cloud app that lets you pick any existing page, set all the titles up front, choose where they go, and generate the whole batch at once.

How it works

  1. Pick a source page — any existing Confluence page becomes the template
  2. Name your copies up front — no more renaming after the fact
  3. Choose a destination — select the space and where in the page tree they should land
  4. Generate — all pages are created and ready to open immediately

A quick example

Say you run monthly product reports. Instead of duplicating and renaming one page at a time, you open Bulk Page Cloner, set your titles, and walk away with:

  • Product Report — Mar 2026
  • Product Report — Apr 2026
  • Product Report — May 2026
  • Product Report — June 2026

All under the right parent page, consistently named, in seconds.

The same pattern works for onboarding docs, project kickoff pages, sprint templates, client folders — anything you find yourself recreating on a schedule.
